### Key Strengths
- **Good walkability** — 7/10 from OSM analysis, driven by mixed-use zoning and sidewalk network
- **Outstanding dining scene** — 89 restaurants within 1.5km, including Ethiopian, Thai, Burmese, Indian, Italian, Mexican, Vietnamese, Korean, and Greek cuisine. Notable nearby: Tara Thai NW (42m), Top Burmese Burma Joy (47m), Siri Indian Cuisine (69m)
- **Excellent transit access** — Portland Streetcar stops (NW 23rd & Marshall at 128m, NW Northrup & 22nd at 173m, NW Lovejoy & 22nd at 256m) plus TriMet bus lines all within 400m — **OSM-verified**
- **Strong healthcare access** — 28 facilities within 1.5km including Walgreens pharmacy at just 38m distance, plus ZoomCare urgent care and specialized clinics
- **Vibrant shopping corridor** — 16 independent retail shops on NW 23rd Ave (Title Nine, Faherty, Marine Layer, Cotopaxi, Arc'teryx, prAna)
- **Good fitness options** — 11 sports/fitness venues including YogaSix (260m), Orangetheory Fitness (318m), and Portland Aikikai
- **Diverse cultural dining** — McMenamins Tavern & Pool and Jo Bar provide evening social venues
- **Local art** — "Large Metal Pigs" public artwork near NW 23rd & Marshall; Howard's Way sculpture by Lee Kelly

### Areas for Improvement
- **Education** — Only 3 schools within 1.5km (Bridges Middle School, Class Academy, Willamette University Portland campus for grad programs); elementary school options remain limited
- **Parks** — Only 3 small playgrounds within 1.5km; no full-size parks or green spaces. The nearest parks are in the Pearl District area
- **Entertainment** — No cinemas, theaters, or music venues within 1.5km. Portland's entertainment district is concentrated downtown (≈3km south)
- **Groceries** — Only 3 options within 1.5km; Zupan's (955m) is the closest full supermarket
